What is Happiness and how can it be measured? Happiness is subject to debate on usage and meaning. People have been trying to measure happiness for centuries. In 1780, the English utilitarian philosopher Jeremy Bentham proposed that as happiness was the primary goal of humans it should be measured as a way of determining how well the government was performing. Since 2012, a World Happiness Report has been published. Happiness is evaluated, as in "How happy are you with your life as a whole?", and in emotional reports, as in "How happy are you now?," and people seem able to use happiness as appropriate in these verbal contexts. Using these measures, the report identifies the countries with the highest levels of happiness. In subjective well-being measures, the primary distinction is between cognitive life evaluations and emotional reports. So, to get a better understanding of this report, a map with data for each country for 2015-2021 has been created, using Tableau.

As mentioned before, happiness is a multidimensional subject that can be affected by a variety of dimensions. We will examine the dimensions of Peace, Education level and Wealth and how they affect the happiness score of each country in the range of years 2015-2021.

So, let's explore happiness and try to understand different aspects of life that affect it.
